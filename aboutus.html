About Us

Founded in 1997, Pitt Model United Nations is a competitive, academic organization at the University of Pittsburgh. The club provides members the opportunity to learn about and discuss current international diplomacies. Each student participating in a conference will assume the role of a delegate representing a country in the United Nations, a business executive at an international corporation, an important historical figure, or even a scientist working in a lab of the future. Students will have the opportunity to debate one another, as well as students from across the country.

Purpose

The Model United Nations Club serves as a vehicle through which students can prepare for and participate in international relations competitions, notably Model United Nations simulations. In these competitions, each student assumes the role of a delegate form the country they are assigned and represents the foreign policy for this country.

In addition, every Fall Pitt's Model UN club works with the University Center for International Studies (UCIS) to host a Model UN conference for nearby high schools. Every club member has the opportunity to be a part of this conference by chairing, staffing, or assissting with background guides. More information about the PittMUN high school conference, please see the High School Conference Page.

Meetings

Pitt MUN meets every Monday at 8:30 PM in 4130 Wesley W. Posvar Hall.
Meetings generally include an update on current events both in the club and around the globe, followed by a lesson on how to be successful in Model UN conferences. Most meetings end with a speaking game, or miniature simulation to reinforce that week's lesson.

Four times a year, before we travel to any conference, we will substitute an in-house simulation for our regular meeting. In-house simulations are short 3-4 hour simulations of a real crisis based Model UN committee session used to train and prepare our club for conferences. Previous topics include Presidential Elections, Executive Board Meetings for Businesses, Game of Thrones Council Meetings, and of course regular UN body committees.

Chairing Experience

Each member of the club also has the opportunity to chair for numerous high school Model United Nations conferences. Pitt MUN holds its own high school conference annually and members have the chance to lead entire committees. Members can also chair at Duquesne University and Westmoreland Community College during their high school conferences, as well as during in-house simulations held within the club several times a semester.

Social Events

Approximately twice a month, the club has a social event. The past social events include:
  • Poker night
  • Causal hangout nights
  • Club dinners
  • Holiday parties
  • Trips to Pittsburgh attractions
  • Bowling
